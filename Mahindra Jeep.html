Mahindra Jeep (CJ / MM540)

About Mahindra Jeep (CJ / MM540) :

*Overview

  • Manufacturer: Mahindra & Mahindra
  • Launch Period: 1950s onwards
  • Models: CJ (Civilian Jeep), MM540
  • Inspired By: Willys Jeep (USA)
  • Engine Options: Diesel engines (high torque)
  • Body Type: Off-road utility vehicle
  • Drive Type: 4x4 (four-wheel drive)

The Mahindra Jeep (CJ / MM540) is one of the most rugged and iconic vehicles in Indian automotive history. Manufactured by Mahindra & Mahindra, these Jeeps were inspired by the original Willys Jeep and became synonymous with strength, reliability, and off-road capability. Introduced in India in the 1950s and popular for several decades, the CJ and MM540 models were widely used by the Indian Army, police forces, farmers, and rural communities. Known for their simple mechanical design and tough ladder-frame chassis, Mahindra Jeeps were built to survive harsh terrains, including mountains, deserts, and muddy village roads. They were powered mainly by diesel engines, offering high torque and excellent durability. The 4x4 capability made them ideal for off-roading and extreme conditions, while their easy maintenance ensured long service life even in remote areas. Though basic in comfort, the CJ and MM540 were highly dependable and versatile, often modified for personal, agricultural, or military use. Even today, these Jeeps enjoy a cult following among off-road enthusiasts and collectors. Restored versions are commonly seen in rallies, adventure drives, and exhibitions.
