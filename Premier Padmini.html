Premier Padmini

About Premier Padmini :

*Overview

  • Manufacturer: Premier Automobiles Limited (PAL)
  • Launch Year: 1973
  • Discontinued: 2001
  • Based On: Fiat 1100D (Italy)
  • Engine Options: Petrol engine (later diesel variants)
  • Nickname: “Padmini” / “Fiat” (commonly called)

The Premier Padmini is one of the most iconic cars in Indian automotive history and holds a special place in the hearts of millions. Manufactured by Premier Automobiles Limited (PAL), the Padmini was launched in 1973 and was based on the Italian Fiat 1100D. With its compact size, elegant European design, and reliable performance, the car quickly became popular among Indian families and taxi operators. It was especially famous in Mumbai, where the black-and-yellow Premier Padmini taxis became a defining part of the city’s identity. The car was powered mainly by a petrol engine, known for its smooth operation and easy maintenance, making it ideal for everyday use. Its rear-wheel-drive setup and sturdy build allowed it to handle Indian road conditions effectively. Though the interior was simple, it offered sufficient comfort for small families and long drives. For many middle-class households, owning a Premier Padmini was a matter of pride and a symbol of upward mobility.
